## 1. Nature of the Service

### 1.1 Evidence-Only System

**THE SERVICE IS AN EVIDENCE-ONLY OBSERVATION SYSTEM.**

The Service:
- Observes and classifies market microstructure reactions
- Provides visualization of order book dynamics
- Generates evidence bundles with cryptographic integrity
- Assesses "belief states" based on reaction patterns

> "看存在没意义，看反应才有意义" — The system watches reactions, not outcomes.

**THE SERVICE DOES NOT PROVIDE:**
- Trading signals or buy/sell recommendations

## 3. Prediction Market Specific Risks

### 3.1 Market Characteristics

Prediction markets have unique characteristics that affect interpretation:

| Characteristic | Implication |
|----------------|-------------|
| Low liquidity | Price movements may not reflect genuine belief changes |
| Thin order books | Large orders can cause disproportionate price impact |
| Manipulation | Markets may be subject to manipulation attempts |
| Volatile spreads | Bid-ask spreads can widen dramatically |
| Event-driven | Sharp movements around news events |

### 3.2 Interpretation Limitations

- A "FRAGILE" or "BROKEN" belief state does NOT mean you should trade
- A "STABLE" belief state does NOT mean the market is correctly priced
- Shock events may be noise rather than signal
- Reaction patterns vary by market, event type, and time of day

### 3.3 Regulatory Uncertainty

Prediction markets operate in evolving regulatory environments:

- Legal status varies by jurisdiction
- Regulations may change without notice
- Access may be restricted in your location
- Past availability does not guarantee future availability

## 5. Evidence Integrity Risks

### 5.1 Evidence Quality

Evidence bundles are assigned quality grades (A, B, C, D) based on:

| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| A | Full data integrity, no gaps |
| B | Minor gaps, evidence still strong |
| C | Significant gaps, use with caution |
| D | Major issues, evidence unreliable |

**Lower grades indicate higher uncertainty.**

### 5.2 Tainted Windows

When data integrity issues are detected:

- Affected time windows are marked as "tainted"
- Alerts from tainted data have capped severity
- Replay determinism may be affected
- Evidence bundles may be incomplete

### 5.3 Cryptographic Limitations

While we use cryptographic hashes for integrity:

- Hashes verify data hasn't changed, not that it was correct initially
- Clock drift or ordering issues may affect determinism
- Upstream data errors are propagated into hashes

## 6. Behavioral Risks

### 6.1 Cognitive Biases

Be aware of these common biases:

| Bias | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Confirmation bias | Seeking evidence that supports existing beliefs |
| Overconfidence | Overestimating your ability to interpret signals |
| Recency bias | Overweighting recent events |
| Hindsight bias | Believing you "knew it all along" after the fact |
| Action bias | Feeling compelled to act when inaction is appropriate |

### 6.2 Misuse Risks

The Service may be misused by:

- Treating evidence as trading signals
- Over-trading based on minor state changes
- Ignoring evidence that contradicts your position
- Using the Service as validation for predetermined decisions

### 6.3 Emotional Risks

Monitoring markets can cause:

- Stress and anxiety
- Compulsive checking behavior
- Emotional decision-making
- Sleep disruption (if monitoring 24/7)
